# Legal Research Guide: Canada and United States

## Overview
Comprehensive guide to conducting legal research across Canadian and US jurisdictions, including case law, statutes, and regulatory compliance.

## Canadian Legal System

### Court Hierarchy

#### Federal Courts
1. **Supreme Court of Canada (SCC)**
   - Final court of appeal
   - Hears ~80 cases/year
   - Binding on all Canadian courts
   - Citations: [Year] SCC [Number]

2. **Federal Court of Appeal**
   - Appeals from Federal Court
   - Tax Court appeals
   - Citations: [Year] FCA [Number]

3. **Federal Court**
   - Federal statutes, intellectual property
   - Maritime law, immigration
   - Citations: [Year] FC [Number]

#### Provincial Courts (Ontario Example)
1. **Court of Appeal for Ontario**
   - Highest provincial appellate court
   - Binding within Ontario
   - Citations: [Year] ONCA [Number]

2. **Superior Court of Justice**
   - General jurisdiction trial court
   - Citations: [Year] ONSC [Number]

3. **Ontario Court of Justice**
   - Provincial offences, family matters
   - Citations: [Year] ONCJ [Number]

### Case Law Research

#### Citation Format
**Supreme Court**: *R. v. Jordan*, 2016 SCC 27, [2016] 1 SCR 631

Components:
- Case name (italicized)
- Year decided [Year]
- Court abbreviation
- Case number
- Optional: Reporter citation

#### Finding Cases

**By Topic**:
1. Start with secondary sources (textbooks, legal encyclopedias)
2. Note key cases cited
3. Use digest services (Canadian Abridgment)
4. Update with citators (KeyCite, Quicklaw)

**By Keyword**:
- Use Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT)
- Proximity searches ("director w/10 liability")
- Field searches (court:SCC AND date:>2020)

#### Reading Cases

**Structure**:
1. **Headnote**: Summary of facts, issues, decision
2. **Facts**: What happened
3. **Issues**: Legal questions
4. **Analysis**: Court's reasoning
5. **Ratio Decidendi**: Binding principle
6. **Obiter Dicta**: Non-binding comments
7. **Disposition**: Final order

**Key Questions**:
- What are the material facts?
- What is the legal issue?
- What rule of law applies?
- How does the court apply the law to facts?
- What is the holding?
- Is this precedent still good law?

### Statutory Interpretation

#### Principles
1. **Plain Meaning Rule**: Words given ordinary meaning
2. **Golden Rule**: Avoid absurd results
3. **Mischief Rule**: What problem was statute addressing?
4. **Modern Approach**: Text, context, and purpose

#### Tools
- **Definitions Section**: Within statute
- **Interpretation Acts**: Federal and provincial
- **Legislative History**: Hansard debates
- **Related Statutes**: *In pari materia*

## United States Legal System

### Court Hierarchy

#### Federal Courts
1. **Supreme Court of the United States (SCOTUS)**
   - Final authority on federal law
   - ~70-80 cases/year
   - Citations: [Volume] U.S. [Page] ([Year])
   - Example: 347 U.S. 483 (1954)

2. **U.S. Courts of Appeals (Circuit Courts)**
   - 13 circuits (11 regional + DC + Federal)
   - Citations: [Volume] F.3d [Page] ([Circuit] [Year])
   - Example: 123 F.3d 456 (9th Cir. 2020)

3. **U.S. District Courts**
   - Trial courts (94 districts)
   - Citations: [Volume] F. Supp. 3d [Page] ([District] [Year])
   - Example: 234 F. Supp. 3d 567 (S.D.N.Y. 2019)

#### State Courts (New York Example)
1. **Court of Appeals**
   - Highest state court
   - Citations: [Volume] N.Y.3d [Page] ([Year])

2. **Appellate Division**
   - Intermediate appellate court
   - Citations: [Volume] A.D.3d [Page] ([Year])

3. **Supreme Court**
   - General trial court (confusingly named)
   - Citations: [Volume] Misc. 3d [Page] ([Year])

## Corporate and Business Law

### Business Formation

#### Canada
**Corporation Types**:
1. **Federal Corporation (CBCA)**
   - Can operate nationwide
   - "Inc." or "Ltd." or "Corp."
   - Directors: Minimum 25% Canadian resident

2. **Provincial Corporation (e.g., OBCA)**
   - Ontario operations
   - Cheaper incorporation
   - May need extra-provincial registration for other provinces

**Requirements**:
- Articles of Incorporation
- Notice of Directors
- Notice of Registered Office
- Corporate Records (minute books)

#### United States
**Entity Types**:
1. **C Corporation**
   - Double taxation
   - Unlimited shareholders
   - Best for: Large businesses, going public

2. **S Corporation**
   - Pass-through taxation
   - Max 100 shareholders
   - Best for: Small businesses avoiding double tax

3. **LLC (Limited Liability Company)**
   - Flexible management
   - Pass-through taxation
   - Best for: Small businesses, real estate

**Requirements**:
- Articles of Incorporation/Organization
- Operating Agreement (LLC)
- Bylaws (Corporation)
- EIN from IRS
- State registrations

### Director and Officer Liability

#### Canada
**Business Judgment Rule**:
- Directors protected if acting:
  - In good faith
  - With reasonable care
  - In best interests of corporation

**Personal Liability**:
- Employee wages (up to 6 months)
- Unremitted taxes
- Environmental violations
- Oppression remedy claims

**Defenses**:
- Due diligence
- Reliance on experts
- Dissent properly recorded

#### United States
**Fiduciary Duties**:
1. **Duty of Care**
   - Informed decision-making
   - Reasonable investigation
   - Active oversight

2. **Duty of Loyalty**
   - No self-dealing
   - Corporate opportunity doctrine
   - Conflict of interest disclosure

**D&O Insurance**:
- Coverage for legal defense
- Settlement payments
- $1-10 million common
- Essential for public companies

### Securities Regulation

#### Canada
**National Instrument 45-106**:
- Prospectus exemptions
- Accredited investor definition:
  - $5 million financial assets
  - $1 million net income
  - $200k individual / $300k combined

**Provincial Regulators**:
- OSC (Ontario)
- ASC (Alberta)
- BCSC (British Columbia)

#### United States
**Securities Act of 1933**:
- Registration requirements
- Prospectus delivery
- **Exemptions**:
  - Regulation D (private placements)
  - Regulation A (mini-IPO)
  - Regulation Crowdfunding

**Accredited Investor**:
- $200k income individual
- $300k combined income
- $1 million net worth (excluding home)
- Certain professionals and entities

**Securities Exchange Act of 1934**:
- Ongoing reporting (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K)
- Insider trading rules
- Proxy regulations

## Tax Law

### Canadian Taxation

#### Corporate Tax
**Federal Rate**: 15% (after abatement and rate reduction)
**Small Business Deduction**: 9% on first $500k active business income
**Combined Rate**: 26.5% general (Ontario), 12.2% SBD

**Key Concepts**:
- Active vs. Passive Income
- GRIP (General Rate Income Pool)
- RDTOH (Refundable Dividend Tax On Hand)
- Integration principle

#### Personal Tax (Federal + Ontario)
- $0-$50,000: ~20%
- $50,000-$100,000: ~30%
- $100,000-$155,000: ~40%
- $155,000-$220,000: ~45%
- $220,000+: ~53.5%

#### GST/HST
- Federal GST: 5%
- Ontario HST: 13% (5% federal + 8% provincial)
- Registration threshold: $30,000 revenue
- Input Tax Credits (ITCs)

### United States Taxation

#### Federal Corporate Tax
**Rate**: 21% flat (since Tax Cuts and Jobs Act 2017)
**State Tax**: 0-12% depending on state
**Combined**: Varies by state (average ~25%)

**C Corp vs. S Corp**:
- C Corp: Corporate tax + dividend tax
- S Corp: Pass-through to shareholders

#### Personal Tax (Federal)
- 10%: $0-$11,000
- 12%: $11,000-$44,725
- 22%: $44,725-$95,375
- 24%: $95,375-$182,100
- 32%: $182,100-$231,250
- 35%: $231,250-$578,125
- 37%: $578,125+

**State Tax**: 0-13.3% (California highest)

#### Sales Tax
- No federal sales tax
- State sales tax: 0-7.25%
- Local sales tax: Additional 0-5%
- Combined: Up to 10%+

## Employment Law

### Canada

#### Employment Standards
**Minimum Requirements** (Ontario):
- Minimum wage: $16.55/hour (2024)
- Overtime: 1.5x after 44 hours/week
- Vacation: 2 weeks minimum (4% pay)
- Public holidays: 9 paid days
- Notice of termination: 1-8 weeks based on length

#### Common Law Notice
- Reasonable notice for termination without cause
- Typically 2-4 weeks per year of service
- **Bardal factors**:
  - Age
  - Length of service
  - Position
  - Availability of similar employment

#### Statutory Deductions
- CPP: 5.95% (matched by employer)
- EI: 1.63% (employer pays 1.4x)
- Income tax: Varies by income

### United States

#### Employment At-Will
- Default rule: Either party can terminate anytime
- **Exceptions**:
  - Contract (employment agreements)
  - Public policy (whistleblowers)
  - Implied contract (employee handbooks)
  - Discrimination laws

#### Minimum Wage
- Federal: $7.25/hour
- State: Varies ($7.25-$16.28)
- Highest: District of Columbia, California, Washington

#### Overtime (FLSA)
- 1.5x regular rate after 40 hours/week
- **Exempt Employees**: Executives, professionals, administrative
- Salary threshold: $684/week ($35,568/year)

#### Benefits
- **FMLA**: 12 weeks unpaid family/medical leave
- **COBRA**: Continued health insurance (18 months)
- **401(k)**: Employer retirement plans
- No statutory vacation or sick leave (federal)

## Intellectual Property

### Trademarks

#### Canada (CIPO)
- Registration term: 10 years (renewable)
- Use in commerce required
- **Classes**: Goods and services classification
- Opposition period: 2 months
- Costs: $330 online application

#### United States (USPTO)
- Registration term: 10 years (renewable)
- Use in commerce or intent-to-use
- **Classes**: International classification system
- Opposition period: 30 days (extensible)
- Costs: $250-$350/class
- Maintenance: Required filings at years 5-6, 9-10

### Copyright

#### Canada
- **Term**: Life + 70 years
- **Automatic**: Upon creation
- **Registration**: Optional but recommended
- **Moral Rights**: Cannot be assigned
- **Fair Dealing**: Limited use exceptions

#### United States
- **Term**: Life + 70 years (works for hire: 95/120 years)
- **Automatic**: Upon fixation
- **Registration**: Required for litigation
- **First Sale Doctrine**: Resale rights
- **Fair Use**: Four-factor test

### Patents

#### Canada
- **Term**: 20 years from filing
- **Requirements**: Novel, useful, non-obvious
- **Maintenance Fees**: Annual from year 2
- **Costs**: $15,000-$30,000 total

#### United States
- **Term**: 20 years from filing
- **First-to-File**: Since March 2013
- **Provisional Application**: 12-month priority
- **Costs**: $5,000-$15,000 (simple), $10,000-$30,000 (complex)

## Legal Research Best Practices

### Research Strategy
1. **Define the Issue**: Narrow legal question
2. **Secondary Sources**: Get overview (treatises, law reviews)
3. **Primary Sources**: Find statutes and cases
4. **Update**: Verify law is current
5. **Analogize**: Apply to your facts
6. **Document**: Track sources

### Boolean Search Tips
- **AND**: Both terms (contract AND breach)
- **OR**: Either term (employee OR worker)
- **NOT**: Exclude term (tax NOT income)
- **Quotes**: Exact phrase ("duty of care")
- **Wildcards**: director* (director, directors, directorship)
- **Proximity**: w/5 (within 5 words)

### Efficient Research
1. **Start Broad**: Get lay of land
2. **Narrow Focus**: Specific jurisdiction and issue
3. **Use Headnotes**: Find relevant parts
4. **Shepardize/KeyCite**: Ensure good law
5. **Check Subsequent History**: Appeals, amendments
6. **Note Dissents**: Potential future changes

## Summary

Effective legal research requires:
1. **Understand Hierarchy**: Know which courts bind which
2. **Primary Sources**: Always verify with original sources
3. **Current Law**: Update research regularly
4. **Multiple Jurisdictions**: Compare approaches
5. **Document Sources**: Maintain research trail
6. **Professional Assistance**: Consult lawyers for specific advice

This guide provides foundational knowledge for legal research across Canada and the United States. Always verify current law and seek professional legal advice for specific situations.
